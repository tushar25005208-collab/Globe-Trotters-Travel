1. Pack Smart, Not Heavy

Rule of thumb: Lay out everything you *think* you need, then put half of it back. Use packing cubes to save space and stay organized.

2. Always Carry Local Cash

While cards are widely accepted, small vendors or taxis in many countries prefer cash. Always have a small amount of the local currency on hand.

3. Backup Your Documents

Scan your passport, visa, and tickets. Keep digital copies on your phone, in your email, and on a cloud service in case the originals are lost or stolen.

4. Learn Basic Phrases

Knowing how to say "Hello," "Thank you," and "Where is the bathroom?" in the local language can drastically improve your interactions.

5. Stay Hydrated and Insured

Never skimp on **travel insurance** and always carry a reusable water bottle. Health and safety are your top priorities.
